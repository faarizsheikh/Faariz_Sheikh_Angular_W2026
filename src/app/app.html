"You'll float, too.

Y–You'll float, too. You'll float, too-hoo. You'll float, too.

You'll float, too! You'll float, too!

You'll float, too!! You'll float, too!! You'll float, too!! You'll float, too!! You'll float, too!! You'll float, too!!

— Fake Georgie to Billy Denbrough, {{favouriteFilm}} ({{yearRelease}})

Why this quote

This line is chilling because of what happens when Pennywise catches his victims. Georgie's fate in the film is especially tragic, which gives the quote emotional weight.

Pennywise's ability to create illusions and manipulate perception adds to the horror. Notice how Georgie's delivery escalates: neutral → smiling → laughing → serious → angry → shouting → yelling. The repetition mirrors psychological pressure.

I am a fan of Stephen King. Works like IT and The Shining explore disturbing themes of fear, guilt, and loss, which makes both the books and films compelling.
